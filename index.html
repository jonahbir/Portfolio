About Me

Welcome to my portfolio. I am Yonas Birhanu, a graduate of the Oromia Development Association (ODA) Special Boarding School. I am a student driven by the intersection of theoretical physics and computational logic. My ultimate goal is to contribute to the upcoming revolution in Quantum Computing, a field that represents the perfect synergy of my passions. To build the necessary technical foundation, I have pursued rigorous training beyond the standard curriculum. I completed a robotics course at STEMpower and participated in AddisCoder, an intensive four-week algorithms program led by Professor Jelani Nelson of UC Berkeley. These experiences, along with my work on platforms like Codeforces and LeetCode, allowed me to master the mathematical and algorithmic "pillars" required for advanced science. My journey is defined by a commitment to "learning by teaching." After mastering the basics of coding, I took on the leadership of my school’s ICT Club, where I taught students the fundamentals of C++, Python, and cybersecurity. To reach a wider audience, I co-organized the AddisCoder Spark bootcamp, an extension of the original program where my team and I taught programming to 62 students over two consecutive years. I also founded and led the Cypher Bootcamp in Ambo, Ethiopia. In partnership with a local college, I organized students into specialized tracks including web development and advanced algorithms, bridging the gap between high school theory and professional skill-sets. This leadership extends to scientific advocacy and community service. I established the Space-Science Club and authored a beginner-friendly book on theoretical physics in Afaan Oromoo to ensure language was not a barrier to curiosity. Furthermore, I organized my school’s inaugural Science Exhibition. By mentoring 16 different student projects, I helped create a model for excellence that was eventually showcased to delegates from the Chinese Embassy. My commitment to impact is also reflected in my humanitarian work; through the charity club, I led a fundraising initiative that raised 76,000 ETB to support the Borana people during the drought. Looking ahead, I plan to pursue an undergraduate degree in Computer Science and Mathematics. I am eager to apply my background in community leadership and technical problem-solving to the global scientific community. Let’s stay curious, courageous, creative, and collaborative!
